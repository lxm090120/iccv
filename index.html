PhysICL Learning In-Context World Physics for Video Generation

Anonymous Submission

Abstract

Recent advances in video generation models have achieved impressive feats in creating personalized, diverse videos. However, these models face a significant barrier: they fail to simulate physically plausible phenomena, as they primarily learn data distributions without accounting for underlying physical laws. Physics engines, on the other hand, excel at precise simulations but are limited to closed-domain scenarios, relying heavily on predefined assets. To address this issue, we introduce PhysICL, a framework that integrates physical simulation priors through in-context learning, which is the first of its kind to seamlessly combine the power of physics engines with generative models. At the core of PhysICL, a physics engine-guided LLM agent autonomously retrieves reference simulations from an asset library and adapts them to user-defined scenes by manipulating the latent space in a context-aware manner through an in-context learning pipeline. This novel decoupling of physical reasoning from visual synthesis allows PhysICL to achieve truly open-domain physical simulation, generating videos with realistic, physically plausible phenomena. Our results surpass existing video generation models lacking physics context, demonstrating significant improvements in quantitative metrics and human preference evaluations. We show that physical parameters can precisely control our model, unlocking unprecedented flexibility and creative control. Furthermore, we conduct experiments on robotics datasets, highlighting the real-world potential of PhysICL. The source code will be released.
